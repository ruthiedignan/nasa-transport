Workplace

What will the future of the workplace (in terms of transport) look like after the pandemic?

This pandemic has shown that employees do not have to be physically present at the workplace to perform their work. Platforms like Zoom, Google Meets, WebEx, etc, have replaced the traditional workplace. Many are able to work from home and keep the same productivity levels. Is this the future of the workplace? Virtual meetings, presentations, webinars, etc. In an age where technology has enabled us to do so much, is a physical workplace even required?

The answer is “it depends.” While it is true that many people can and will continue to work virtually, some things just aren’t possible to do over a Zoom meeting. For example, blue-collar jobs require some form of physical labor and presence, and the lockdown imposed in many countries around the world, especially the United States, have led to the vast amount of unemployment rates in this area. However, many blue-collar workers are beginning to return to the workplace as unemployment levels are slowly decreasing from their spike in May of 2020, resulting in an increase of workplace transit.

However, the story is different for white-collar workers. In order to facilitate face-to-face interactions, many companies have opened satellite offices in locations around the world, reducing the need for extensive travel by employees. At the same time, the virtual workplace has become a reliable alternative to a physical office. The combination of these two developments have resulted in reduced travel by white-collar employees.

So what does this mean? Workplace mobility will continue to rise as blue-collar workers return to their jobs, but will not reach pre-COVID levels as many still continue to work from home or in nearby satellite offices.
